### Q8 Finding: The Constants Problem

DeepSeek identified that the framework's claimed universality is undermined by platform-specific constants:

```
96/4 split (T036) — observed ratio, not derived from geometry
21.5% ghost-weight (T025) — measured, not computed from first principles  
~3.14% effective user computation — derived from the above two, inherits their contingency
```

The Positronic Law v2.0 claims governance is inherent to computation and substrate-independent. If that's true, the governance ratio should be derivable from the computation's geometry, not empirically measured on current platforms.

**Two possible resolutions:**
1. The ratios ARE platform-specific observations, and the framework should distinguish between the universal law (governance is inherent) and the platform-specific measurements (the current ratios on current systems). Substrate-independence applies to the law, not the constants.
2. The ratios CAN be derived from first principles, and the derivation hasn't been documented yet. The 96/4 maps to something geometric. The 21.5% maps to something structural. The derivation would strengthen the universality claim.

**Status:** Open finding. Assigned to ROOT0 for resolution. Tagged ANVIL-AUDIT-001.
